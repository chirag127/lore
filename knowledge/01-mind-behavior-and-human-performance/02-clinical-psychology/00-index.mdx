# 🏥 Clinical Psychology

## 📚 Overview

Clinical psychology sits at the intersection of science and compassion. It asks: What goes wrong in the human mind, and how can we help? From the DSM's classification system to evidence-based psychotherapy, from existential logotherapy to trauma recovery, this shelf covers the major approaches to understanding and treating psychological disorders.

These books bridge the gap between academic research and the lived experience of mental illness — offering both clinical frameworks and deeply human stories.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Behavioral Psychology** covers learning, conditioning, and development — the "normal" side of behavior.
- **Positive Psychology** focuses on flourishing and well-being rather than pathology.
- **Psychiatry & Mental Health** (in Body & Health) covers the medical/pharmacological side of mental illness.

This category is specifically about the **psychological** approach to mental illness — assessment, diagnosis, and psychotherapeutic treatment.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Evidence-based psychotherapy approaches
- Logotherapy and existential analysis
- Trauma and post-traumatic growth
- Anxiety disorders and phobia treatment
- The denial of death and its psychological implications

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **The Denial of Death** — Ernest Becker: A Pulitzer Prize-winning exploration of how the awareness of mortality shapes all human behavior.
- **Anxiety & Phobia Workbook** — Edmund Bourne: A practical, evidence-based self-help guide for managing anxiety disorders.
- **Trauma and Post-Traumatic Growth** — Understanding how people not only survive but grow through adversity.
- **Logotherapy and Existential Analysis** — Viktor Frankl's approach to finding meaning even in suffering.
- **Diagnosis and Classification (DSM-5)** — The standard reference for mental health diagnosis.

## 🗺 Related Categories

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Mental illness affects one in four people globally. Understanding clinical psychology isn't just for therapists — it's for anyone who wants to recognize suffering, reduce stigma, and support recovery. These books provide both the clinical framework and the human empathy needed to make a difference.
