# 🩺 Medicine & Clinical Science

## 📚 Overview

Medicine is one of humanity's greatest achievements — and its history is filled with both triumph and tragedy. This shelf covers the science of clinical medicine, the history of medical discovery, and the social dimensions of healthcare.

From Siddhartha Mukherjee's biography of cancer to Harriet Washington's exposé of medical racism, from Steven Johnson's cholera investigation to the broader landscape of clinical science, these books illuminate how medicine works — and sometimes doesn't.

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **The Emperor of All Maladies** — Siddhartha Mukherjee: A Pulitzer Prize-winning biography of cancer — its history, treatment, and the ongoing quest for a cure.
- **Medical Apartheid** — Harriet Washington: A devastating history of medical experimentation on Black Americans.
- **The Ghost Map** — Steven Johnson: The story of London's cholera outbreak and how it changed science and cities forever.
