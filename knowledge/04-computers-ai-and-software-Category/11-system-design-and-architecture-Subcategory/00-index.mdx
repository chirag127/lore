# 🏛️ System Design & Architecture

## 📚 Overview

How do you design systems that serve millions of users? This shelf covers the architecture of large-scale distributed systems: from Martin Kleppmann's Designing Data-Intensive Applications to Alex Xu's System Design Interview, from distributed caching and messaging to scalability patterns and high availability.

These books are essential for anyone who designs, builds, or operates systems at scale — from startup engineers to architects at major tech companies.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Software Design Principles** covers software-level design patterns.
- **Networking & Distributed Systems** covers networking protocols.
- **DevOps & Platform Engineering** covers deployment and operations.

This category focuses on the **system-level architecture** — how to design and build large-scale distributed systems.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Designing data-intensive applications
- Distributed databases and CAP theorem
- Caching strategies and distributed caching
- Distributed messaging systems
- Data streaming and event processing
- Scalability patterns and load balancing
- High availability architecture
- Consensus and replication
- System design interview frameworks

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Designing Data-Intensive Applications** — Martin Kleppmann: The definitive guide to the systems that store and process data at scale.
- **System Design Interview, Vol. 1** — Alex Xu: A practical framework for tackling system design questions.
- **Database Internals** — Alex Petrov: How databases work under the hood — storage, indexing, and query processing.
- **The Art of Scalability** — Martin Abbott: A systematic approach to scaling organizations and technology.
- **Designing Distributed Systems** — Brendan Burns: Patterns and paradigms for scalable, reliable services.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Every major tech product is a distributed system. Understanding how to design systems that scale, remain available, and handle failures gracefully is the most valuable skill for senior engineers and architects.
