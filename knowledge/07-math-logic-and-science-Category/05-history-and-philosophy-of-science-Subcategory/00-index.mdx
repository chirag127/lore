# 🔬 History & Philosophy of Science

## 📚 Overview

Science is humanity's most powerful tool for understanding the world — but how does it actually work? This shelf covers the history and philosophy of science: how scientific revolutions happen, what counts as scientific knowledge, and the philosophical questions that science raises.

It includes Thomas Kuhn's Structure of Scientific Revolutions, Paul Feyerabend's Against Method, and works on the philosophy of physics, biology, and mind.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Philosophy** covers the philosophy of mind and consciousness.
- **Physics**, **Biology**, etc. cover the practice of specific sciences.
- **Mathematics** covers the philosophy of mathematics.

This category focuses on **how science works** — its methods, history, and philosophical foundations.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- History of scientific revolutions
- The structure of scientific revolutions: paradigm shifts
- Against Method: Paul Feyerabend's anarchistic theory of knowledge

- Philosophy of physics, biology, and mind

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **The Structure of Scientific Revolutions** — Thomas Kuhn: How science really works — through paradigm shifts, not steady accumulation.
- **Against Method** — Paul Feyerabend: A provocative argument that there is no single scientific method.
- **Scientific Method and Paradigm Shifts** — The logic and history of scientific change.
- **History of Scientific Revolutions** — How major scientific ideas emerged and transformed our understanding.
- **Philosophy of Physics, Biology, and Mind** — The philosophical questions raised by modern science.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Understanding how science works helps you evaluate scientific claims, appreciate the provisional nature of scientific knowledge, and think more clearly about the relationship between science and other ways of knowing.
