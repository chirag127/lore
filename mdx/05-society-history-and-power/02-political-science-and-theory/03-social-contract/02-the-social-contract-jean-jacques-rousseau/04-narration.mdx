Welcome to this exploration of The Social Contract by Jean-Jacques Rousseau.

Rousseau rejects natural right to authority and argues for a pact among citizens as the legitimate source of sovereign power, introducing the concept of the "general will." The treatise profoundly influenced the French Revolution.
