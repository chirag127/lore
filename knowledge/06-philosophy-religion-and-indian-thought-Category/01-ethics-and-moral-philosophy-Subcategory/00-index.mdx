# ⚖️ Ethics & Moral Philosophy

## 📚 Overview

How should we live? What is the good life? What do we owe to each other? Ethics — the study of right and wrong — is the oldest and most practical branch of philosophy.

This shelf covers the foundational texts of Western ethics: Aristotle's Nicomachean Ethics and Eudemian Ethics, and the broader tradition of virtue ethics that flows from them.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Eastern Philosophy** covers Eastern ethical traditions.
- **Public Policy & Law** covers applied ethics and policy.
- **Philosophy** covers meta-ethics and the philosophy of morality.

This category focuses on **normative ethics** — the theories that tell us how to live well.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Nicomachean Ethics: Aristotle's masterwork on the good life
- Eudemian Ethics: Aristotle's other major ethical treatise
- Virtue ethics: character, habit, and the cultivation of excellence
- The good life: eudaimonia and human flourishing

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Nicomachean Ethics** — Aristotle: The foundational text of Western ethics — what it means to live well and be a good person.
- **The Eudemian Ethics** — Aristotle: A companion to the Nicomachean Ethics with additional insights on virtue and happiness.
- **Virtue Ethics** — The tradition of ethics focused on character and virtue rather than rules or consequences.

## 🗺 Related Categories

- **Philosophy** — Meta-ethics and philosophy of morality
- **Eastern Philosophy** — Eastern ethical traditions
- **Western Philosophy** — The broader Western philosophical tradition
- **Religion & Spirituality** — Religious ethics

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Ethics is the most practical branch of philosophy. Every decision you make involves questions of right and wrong, good and bad, virtue and vice. These books provide the frameworks for thinking clearly about how to live.
