## Overview

*The Little Book of Common Sense Investing* (first published 2007,
updated 2017) by John C. Bogle, founder of Vanguard, is the definitive
case for index investing. Bogle argues that the stock market's return
minus the cost of investing equals the investor's return. Since active
management carries higher costs, index funds that simply buy and hold
the market deliver superior long-term results.

The book is built on decades of empirical evidence showing that the
vast majority of actively managed funds underperform their benchmark
indexes, especially after fees and taxes.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Costs matter more than anything else.** The expense ratio is the
   single most reliable predictor of future fund performance.

2. **Index funds win.** Over any meaningful time period, low-cost index
   funds outperform the majority of active funds.

3. **Reversion to the mean is inevitable.** Yesterday's top-performing
   fund is rarely tomorrow's. Past performance does not predict future
   returns.

4. **The magic of compounding works both ways.** Low costs compound in
   your favor. High costs compound against you.

5. **Stay the course.** Time in the market, not timing the market, is
   the key to long-term investment success.

## Final Verdict

The most important investing book ever written for individual investors.
Bogle's evidence is overwhelming and his argument is irrefutable. Every
investor should read this book before buying a single fund.

**Rating: 9/10** — The foundational text of rational investing.
