# 🔬 Cognitive & Behavioral Psychology

## 📚 Overview

Cognitive psychology is the science of the mind's machinery. How do we perceive the world? How do we form memories, focus attention, process language, and make decisions? And what happens when these systems break down?

This shelf covers the core cognitive processes — from perception and pattern recognition to memory and executive function — along with the neuroscience of emotion, motivation, and psychopharmacology. It includes some of the most celebrated books ever written about the human mind.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Behavioral Psychology** focuses on observable behavior and learning mechanisms.
- **Cognitive Biases & Rationality** covers systematic errors in judgment and decision-making.
- **Social Psychology** examines how cognition operates in social contexts.
- **Neuroscience** (in Math, Logic & Science) covers the biological hardware at a deeper level.

This category is about **how the mind processes information** — the cognitive software running on the brain's hardware.

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Thinking, Fast and Slow** — Daniel Kahneman: The Nobel laureate's masterwork on the two systems that drive the way we think.
- **The Man Who Mistook His Wife for a Hat** — Oliver Sacks: A neurologist's deeply human case studies of people with rare neurological disorders.
- **Predictably Irrational** — Dan Ariely: How hidden forces systematically shape our seemingly rational decisions.
- **Range** — David Epstein: Why generalists triumph in a specialized world — a cognitive science perspective.
- **Brain-Behavior Relationships** — Understanding how neural structures give rise to mental functions.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Understanding how your mind processes information is the foundation of all learning, communication, and decision-making. These books reveal the hidden machinery of thought — and show you how to work with it rather than against it.
