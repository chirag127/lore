# 📈 Investment Theory & Principles

## 📚 Overview

How should you invest? This is one of the most consequential questions in personal finance and institutional management. This shelf covers the major investment philosophies and strategies — from Benjamin Graham's value investing to modern portfolio theory, from Warren Buffett's letters to quantitative equity strategies.

With over 40 books, this is one of the richest categories in BookAtlas. It includes the Intelligent Investor, Security Analysis, One Up on Wall Street, Margin of Safety, and many more classics that every serious investor should read.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Behavioral Finance** covers the psychology of investing.
- **Personal Finance** covers personal money management.
- **Finance** covers financial instruments and markets.

This category focuses on **investment philosophy and strategy** — how to think about investing and what approaches work.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Value investing philosophy and practice
- Growth investing and quality investing
- Modern portfolio theory and asset allocation
- Fundamental analysis and security analysis
- Hedge fund strategies and market wizards
- Real estate investment
- Quantitative equity strategies
- The Bitcoin standard and cryptocurrency
- Dollar-cost averaging and long-term investing

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **The Intelligent Investor** — Benjamin Graham: The bible of value investing, with commentary by Jason Zweig.
- **Security Analysis** — Benjamin Graham & David Dodd: The foundational text of fundamental analysis.
- **One Up on Wall Street** — Peter Lynch: How individual investors can beat the pros by using what they already know.
- **Margin of Safety** — Seth Klarman: A rare and brilliant treatise on value investing by one of its greatest practitioners.
- **Essays of Warren Buffett** — Lawrence Cunningham: The collected wisdom of the world's greatest investor, organized by topic.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Investing is the most reliable path to building wealth over the long term. But most investors underperform because they lack a sound philosophy and the discipline to stick with it. These books provide both the philosophy and the evidence to invest wisely.
