For decades, experts made terrible predictions with unjustified confidence. Then Philip Tetlock launched the Good Judgment Project, a multi-year tournament that proved some people could predict the future with remarkable accuracy. *Superforecasting* tells the story of these "superforecasters"—ordinary people who developed extraordinary skill at prediction—and reveals the thinking habits that make them successful.

The book shows that accurate forecasting is not about having a crystal ball. It's about probabilistic thinking, intellectual humility, breaking problems down, updating beliefs in response to evidence, and learning from mistakes. These are skills that can be developed, and Tetlock and Gardner provide a practical guide to developing them.
