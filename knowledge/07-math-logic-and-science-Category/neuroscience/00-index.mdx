# 🧠 Neuroscience

## 📚 Overview

The brain is the most complex object in the known universe. Neuroscience is the science of understanding it: how it's built, how it works, and how it gives rise to thought, emotion, and behavior.

This shelf covers the major areas: neuroanatomy and brain architecture, cognitive neuroscience and decision-making, and neuropharmacology and behavioral neuroscience.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Cognitive & Behavioral Psychology** covers the cognitive processes that neuroscience explains.
- **Psychiatry & Mental Health** covers the clinical treatment of brain disorders.
- **Artificial Intelligence** covers computational models of neural function.

This category focuses on the **biological brain** — its structure, function, and chemistry.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Neuroanatomy: the structure and organization of the brain
- Cognitive neuroscience: how the brain gives rise to thought and decision-making
- Neuropharmacology: how chemicals affect brain function and behavior

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Neuroanatomy and Brain Architecture** — The structure and organization of the brain, from neurons to neural circuits.
- **Cognitive Neuroscience and Decision-Making** — How the brain makes decisions, from simple choices to complex judgments.
- **Neuropharmacology and Behavioral Neuroscience** — How neurotransmitters, drugs, and chemicals affect brain function and behavior.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Understanding the brain is the final frontier of science. Neuroscience is revealing the biological basis of everything we think, feel, and do — and transforming our understanding of what it means to be human.
