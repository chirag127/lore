# 🌐 Macroeconomics

## 📚 Overview

Macroeconomics studies the economy as a whole — growth, inflation, unemployment, trade, and the policies that governments use to manage these forces. This shelf covers the major topics in macroeconomics: fiscal and monetary policy, international trade, currency markets, globalization, and emerging market investing.

Understanding macroeconomics is essential for making sense of the news, evaluating policy debates, and making informed decisions about your career, investments, and business.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Microeconomics** covers individual markets and firms.
- **Finance** covers financial instruments and markets.
- **Development Economics** covers economic development in low-income countries.

This category focuses on the **big picture** — the forces that shape entire economies.

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Currency Markets and FX Strategy** — How foreign exchange markets work and how to trade them.

- **Globalization and Its Critics** — The debate over free trade, capital flows, and economic integration.
- **Trade Policy and Tariffs** — The economics and politics of international trade.
- **Emerging Market Investing** — The opportunities and risks of investing in developing economies.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Macroeconomic forces affect everything from your job prospects to your investment returns to the price of groceries. Understanding macroeconomics helps you make better decisions in an interconnected world economy.
