# 🏛️ Western Philosophy

## 📚 Overview

Western philosophy begins with the ancient Greeks and continues through the present day. This shelf covers the major periods and movements: ancient Greek and Roman philosophy (Plato, Aristotle, the Stoics), existentialism and phenomenology (Kierkegaard, Heidegger, Sartre), and the modern Stoic revival.

It includes Marcus Aurelius's Meditations, Nietzsche's Beyond Good and Evil and Zarathustra, Plato's Republic and Symposium, Viktor Frankl's Man's Search for Meaning, and Ryan Holiday's modern Stoic guides.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Ancient Greek philosophy: Plato's Republic and Symposium
- Ancient Roman philosophy: Stoicism (Seneca, Epictetus, Marcus Aurelius)
- Nietzsche: Beyond Good and Evil, Zarathustra, Genealogy of Morals
- Existentialism: Viktor Frankl's Man's Search for Meaning
- Modern Stoicism: Ryan Holiday's The Daily Stoic and The Obstacle Is the Way
- The Enchiridion: Epictetus's handbook of Stoic practice

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Meditations** — Marcus Aurelius: The private journal of a Roman emperor — the most accessible and practical Stoic text.
- **Man's Search for Meaning** — Viktor Frankl: A Holocaust survivor's testament to the human capacity to find meaning in suffering.
- **The Republic** — Plato: The foundational text of Western philosophy — justice, the ideal state, and the nature of reality.
- **Letters from a Stoic** — Seneca: Practical wisdom from one of Rome's greatest Stoic philosophers.
- **The Daily Stoic** — Ryan Holiday: 366 meditations on wisdom, perseverance, and the art of living.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Western philosophy is the intellectual foundation of Western civilization. Its ideas about reason, justice, virtue, and meaning continue to shape how we think, govern, and live. These books provide direct access to the greatest minds in the Western tradition.
