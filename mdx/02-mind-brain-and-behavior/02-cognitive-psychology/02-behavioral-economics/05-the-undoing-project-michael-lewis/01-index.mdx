Michael Lewis, the master of narrative nonfiction, turns his attention to the story behind one of the most important scientific collaborations of the 20th century: the partnership between Daniel Kahneman and Amos Tversky. Their work on heuristics and biases fundamentally changed how we understand human judgment and laid the foundation for behavioral economics.

The book is as much about the extraordinary friendship between these two brilliant, complicated men as it is about their science. Lewis shows how their unique partnership—a combination of Kahneman's intuitive creativity and Tversky's rigorous logic—produced insights that neither could have achieved alone. It's a story about how ideas are born, how collaborations work, and why the relationship between two people can change the world.
