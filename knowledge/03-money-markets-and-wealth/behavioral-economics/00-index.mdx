# 🧠 Behavioral Economics

## 📚 Overview

Classical economics assumes humans are rational actors. Behavioral economics knows better. This field studies how psychological, cognitive, and emotional factors systematically influence economic decisions — and why we consistently make choices that contradict our own interests.

This shelf covers the key findings in behavioral economics, from heuristics and biases in economic behavior to the broader implications for policy, business, and personal finance.
