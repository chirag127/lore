# 🧠 Psychiatry & Mental Health

## 📚 Overview

Psychiatry is the medical specialty devoted to the diagnosis, prevention, and treatment of mental disorders. This shelf covers the medical approach to mental health — from the neuroscience of psychiatric conditions to the clinical practice of psychiatric care.

## 🎯 Scope

| Included | Excluded |
|----------|----------|
| Psychiatric diagnosis and classification | Psychotherapy (→ Clinical Psychology) |
| Neuropsychiatry | Positive psychology (→ Positive Psychology) |
| Mental health from a medical perspective | Social psychology (→ Social Psychology) |

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Clinical Psychology** covers psychotherapy and psychological approaches to mental illness.
- **Positive Psychology** covers well-being and flourishing.
- **Neuroscience** (in Math, Logic & Science) covers the basic neuroscience of brain function.

This category focuses on the **medical and neuroscientific** approach to mental health.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Neuropsychiatric case studies
- The neuroscience of mental disorders
- Psychiatric diagnosis and treatment

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **The Man Who Mistook His Wife for a Hat** — Oliver Sacks: A neurologist's deeply human case studies of people with rare neurological and psychiatric conditions.

## 🗺 Related Categories

- **Clinical Psychology** — Psychotherapy and psychological treatment
- **Neuroscience** — The neuroscience of brain and behavior
- **Positive Psychology** — Well-being and mental health promotion
- **Medicine & Clinical Science** — General clinical medicine

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Mental illness affects hundreds of millions of people worldwide. Understanding the medical approach to mental health — diagnosis, treatment, and neuroscience — is essential for reducing stigma and improving care.
