# 🏗️ Software Design Principles

## 📚 Overview

Good software design is the difference between a system that evolves gracefully and one that collapses under its own weight. This shelf covers the major design principles and patterns: SOLID principles, design patterns (Gang of Four), domain-driven design, event-driven architecture, microservices, and clean architecture.

From Robert Martin's Clean Architecture to Eric Evans's Domain-Driven Design, from Sam Newman's Building Microservices to the Gang of Four's Design Patterns, these books provide the vocabulary and frameworks for designing software that lasts.

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Clean Architecture** — Robert C. Martin: A craftsman's guide to software structure and design.
- **Design Patterns** — Gamma, Helm, Johnson, Vlissides: The Gang of Four's classic catalog of reusable design patterns.
- **Domain-Driven Design** — Eric Evans: Tackling complexity in the heart of software through strategic design.
- **Building Microservices** — Sam Newman: Designing fine-grained systems that scale and evolve.
- **Fundamentals of Software Architecture** — Mark Richards: An engineering approach to architecture decisions.
