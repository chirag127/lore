# 🔧 Engineering & Technology

## 📚 Overview

Engineering is the application of science to solve real-world problems. This shelf covers the broad landscape of engineering: from circuit theory and electronics to mechanical and structural engineering, from renewable energy to biomedical devices, from robotics to semiconductor manufacturing.

These books span the full range of engineering disciplines, providing both foundational knowledge and specialized expertise.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Software Engineering** covers software development.
- **Computer Science** covers computer hardware architecture.
- **Artificial Intelligence** covers AI and robotics software.

This category focuses on the **physical and electrical** engineering disciplines.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Circuit theory and electronic design
- Classical mechanics and structural analysis
- Embedded systems and microcontrollers
- Energy storage and grid systems
- Renewable energy systems
- Biomedical and neural engineering
- Robotics and autonomous vehicles
- Semiconductor materials and manufacturing
- Signal processing and communications
- Thermodynamics and heat transfer
- Transportation systems and urban infrastructure

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **The Art of Electronics** — Horowitz & Hill: The definitive guide to electronic circuit design.
- **The Prize** — Daniel Yergin: The epic quest for oil, money, and power — a history of the energy industry.
- **Circuit Theory and Electronic Design** — The foundations of electrical engineering.
- **Renewable Energy Systems** — Solar, wind, and the technologies powering the clean energy transition.
- **Embedded Systems and Microcontrollers** — How to build computing systems that interact with the physical world.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Engineering is how we build the physical world. From the phone in your pocket to the power grid that lights your home, engineering makes modern life possible. These books provide the knowledge to understand and build the systems that shape our world.
