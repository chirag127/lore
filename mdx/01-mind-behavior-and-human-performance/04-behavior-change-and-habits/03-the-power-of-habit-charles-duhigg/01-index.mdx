The Power of Habit is the foundational text of the modern habits literature. Charles Duhigg, a Pulitzer Prize-winning investigative reporter for the New York Times, translates decades of neurological and psychological research into an accessible framework for understanding why habits exist and how they can be reshaped. The book spent over 60 weeks on the New York Times bestseller list and has sold over 4 million copies worldwide, establishing Duhigg as the leading popularizer of habit science. Its core insight — that every habit follows a three-step loop of cue, routine, and reward — has become the dominant mental model for behavior change in both personal development and organizational management.
