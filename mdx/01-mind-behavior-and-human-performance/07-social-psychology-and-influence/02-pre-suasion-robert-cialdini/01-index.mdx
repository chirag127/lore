Pre-Suasion is the companion piece to *Influence* — not a sequel but a prequel. Where *Influence* identified what makes people say yes, *Pre-Suasion* investigates the moment *before* the ask: how expert persuaders channel attention, prime associations, and shape the psychological context so that the message lands on fertile ground.

Cialdini draws on decades of research — his own and others' — to argue that the most effective communicators think less about what they say and more about what they establish *before* they say it. The book introduces the concept of "privileged moments" where a single well-timed word or image can redirect an audience's entire frame of reference. It also extends his framework with the Unity principle and a new chapter on post-suasion: what to do after the audience has agreed to lock in lasting commitment.
