David Eagleman, a neuroscientist at Stanford, takes readers on a tour of the unconscious brain—the vast neural machinery that operates below the level of conscious awareness. The book's central thesis is that the conscious mind is not the CEO of the brain but more like a press secretary, narrating decisions that were already made by unconscious neural processes.

Through vivid case studies and thought experiments, Eagleman explores what brain science reveals about free will, personal responsibility, perception, memory, and the nature of the self. The book challenges our most basic assumptions about who we are and why we do what we do.
