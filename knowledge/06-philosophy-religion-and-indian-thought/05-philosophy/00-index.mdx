# 🤔 Philosophy

## 📚 Overview

Philosophy is the foundation of all inquiry. It asks the deepest questions: What can we know? What is real? Do we have free will? What is consciousness? How should we live?

This shelf covers the major branches of philosophy: epistemology (the study of knowledge), metaphysics (the study of reality), philosophy of mind, philosophy of science, logic, and political philosophy. It includes Thomas Kuhn's Structure of Scientific Revolutions and David Byrne's How Music Works.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Ethics & Moral Philosophy** covers moral philosophy.
- **Eastern Philosophy** covers Eastern traditions.
- **Western Philosophy** covers the history of Western philosophy.
- **Religion & Spirituality** covers religious thought.

This category focuses on **core philosophical questions** that cut across traditions.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Epistemology: the nature of knowledge and justified true belief
- Free will and determinism
- Philosophy of mind: consciousness, personal identity, the mind-body problem
- Philosophy of science and scientific explanation
- The structure of scientific revolutions: paradigm shifts
- Formal and informal logic
- Language, meaning, and truth
- Political philosophy: liberalism, communitarianism, libertarianism
- Rationalism vs. empiricism

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **The Structure of Scientific Revolutions** — Thomas Kuhn: How science really works — through paradigm shifts, not steady accumulation.
- **Epistemology of Disagreement** — What should you do when someone disagrees with you?
- **Free Will and Determinism** — Do we have free will, or is everything determined?
- **Philosophy of Consciousness** — The hard problem: why is there subjective experience?
- **Formal Logic: Propositional and Predicate** — The tools of rigorous reasoning.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Philosophy teaches you how to think clearly about the deepest questions. Its tools — logic, argumentation, conceptual analysis — are valuable in every domain of human inquiry.
