# 📊 Statistics & Probability

## 📚 Overview

In a world drowning in data, statistics is the lifeboat. This shelf covers the major topics: Bayesian and frequentist inference, probability distributions, experimental design and hypothesis testing, and stochastic processes and Markov chains.

From Charles Wheelen's Naked Statistics to Peter Bruce's Practical Statistics for Data Scientists, these books provide the tools for making sense of data and uncertainty.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Bayesian statistics: updating beliefs with evidence
- Frequentist statistical inference: hypothesis testing and confidence intervals
- Discrete and continuous probability distributions
- Experimental design and hypothesis testing
- Stochastic processes and Markov chains
- Naked statistics: stripping the dread from data
- Practical statistics for data scientists

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Naked Statistics** — Charles Wheelen: Stripping the dread from data — an accessible introduction to statistics.
- **Practical Statistics for Data Scientists** — Peter Bruce: The statistical methods that matter most for data science.
- **Bayesian Statistics** — The framework for updating beliefs in light of new evidence.
- **Introduction to Probability** — Joseph Blitzstein: A rigorous but accessible introduction to probability theory.
- **Experimental Design and Hypothesis Testing** — How to design experiments and draw valid conclusions from data.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Statistics is the science of learning from data. In an age of information overload, the ability to think statistically — to distinguish signal from noise, to quantify uncertainty, to draw valid conclusions — is more important than ever.
