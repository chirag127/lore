# 🔍 Mystery & Crime Fiction

## 📚 Overview

Who did it? Why? And how will they be caught? Mystery and crime fiction are among the most popular genres in literature. This shelf covers the major subgenres: detective fiction, psychological thrillers, and true crime narrative.

## 🎯 Scope

| Included | Excluded |
|----------|----------|
| Detective fiction (Agatha Christie, Conan Doyle) | Literary criticism (→ Literary Criticism) |
| Psychological thriller | Historical biography (→ Historical Biography) |
| True crime narrative | Genre fiction (→ Genre Fiction) |

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Literary Criticism** covers the analysis of crime fiction.
- **Historical Biography & Memoir** covers true crime biography.
- **Genre Fiction** covers crime mythology.

This category focuses on **crime fiction** as a literary genre.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Detective fiction: the classic whodunit
- psychological thriller: crime from the criminal's perspective
- True crime narrative: real crimes told with literary skill

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Detective Fiction** — The art of the puzzle: from Sherlock Holmes to modern detective stories.
- **Psychological Thriller** — Crime from the inside: the criminal's mind as literary territory.
- **True Crime Narrative** — Real crimes told with the tools of literary nonfiction.

Crime fiction is the most popular genre in literature. Understanding its conventions and innovations helps you appreciate the craft of storytelling at its most suspenseful.
