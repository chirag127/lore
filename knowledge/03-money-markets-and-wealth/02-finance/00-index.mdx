# 💹 Finance

## 📚 Overview

Finance is the engine of the modern economy. It determines how capital flows, how risk is priced, and how businesses fund their growth. This shelf covers the technical side of finance — financial instruments, market microstructure, risk management, and the history of financial crises.

From options theory to market crashes, from banking history to startup valuation, these books provide the technical foundation for understanding how financial markets work.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Investment Theory** covers investment philosophy and strategy.
- **Behavioral Finance** covers the psychology of financial decision-making.
- **Macroeconomics** covers the macroeconomic forces that affect financial markets.

This category focuses on the **technical mechanics** of finance — instruments, markets, and institutions.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Options theory and pricing models
- Futures, forwards, and swaps
- Algorithmic trading and market microstructure
- Risk management: VaR, CVaR, tail risk
- History of banking and central banking
- Market crashes, panics, and crises
- Corporate bonds and credit analysis
- Startup valuation and deal structuring
- Yield curve, duration, and interest rate risk

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Options Theory and Pricing Models** — The mathematical foundations of options pricing, including Black-Scholes and beyond.
- **Market Crashes, Panics, and Crises** — A history of financial crises and what they teach us about market dynamics.
- **Risk Management: VaR, CVaR, Tail Risk** — Quantitative approaches to measuring and managing financial risk.
- **Algorithmic Trading and Market Microstructure** — How modern electronic markets work and how algorithms shape price discovery.
- **History of Banking and Central Banking** — The evolution of financial institutions from medieval money-lenders to modern central banks.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Finance is the plumbing of the modern economy. Understanding how financial instruments work, how markets set prices, and how risk is managed is essential for anyone in business, investing, or public policy.
