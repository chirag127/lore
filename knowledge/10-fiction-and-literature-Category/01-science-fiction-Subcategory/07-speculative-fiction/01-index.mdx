# 🌌 Speculative Fiction

## 📚 Overview

Speculative fiction is the umbrella genre that encompasses science fiction, fantasy, and everything in between. This shelf covers the works that don't fit neatly into a single genre — from Frank Herbert's Dune to the broader tradition of speculative storytelling.

## 🎯 Scope

| Included | Excluded |
|----------|----------|
| Cross-genre speculative fiction | Pure science fiction (→ Science Fiction) |
| Genre-defining masterworks | Pure fantasy (→ Fantasy) |
| The broader tradition of speculative storytelling | Literary criticism (→ Literary Criticism) |

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Science Fiction** covers science-based speculative fiction.
- **Fantasy** covers magical speculative fiction.
- **Literary Criticism** covers speculative fiction theory.

This category focuses on **genre-crossing speculative fiction**.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Dune: Frank Herbert's masterpiece of speculative fiction
- The broader tradition of speculative storytelling

## ⭐ Essential Books

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Speculative fiction expands the boundaries of what's imaginable. The best works in this tradition create entire worlds that illuminate our own — and challenge us to think differently about the future.
