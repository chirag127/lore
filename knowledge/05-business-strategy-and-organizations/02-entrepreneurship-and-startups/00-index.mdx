# 🚀 Entrepreneurship & Startups

## 📚 Overview

Starting a company is one of the hardest things a person can do. This shelf covers the full journey: from the lean startup methodology and customer development to fundraising and founder psychology, from bootstrapping to scaling, from the E-Myth to Zero to One.

With over 20 books, this is one of the richest categories in BookAtlas. It includes Eric Ries's Lean Startup, Peter Thiel's Zero to One, Ben Horowitz's Hard Thing About Hard Things, and many more essential texts for founders.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Business Strategy** covers corporate strategy for established businesses.
- **Leadership** covers leading teams and organizations.
- **Marketing & Brand Strategy** covers go-to-market strategy.

This category focuses on the **entrepreneurial journey** — starting and growing a new business.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Lean startup methodology: build-measure-learn
- Customer development and product discovery
- Fundraising: pitching, term sheets, and investor relations
- Founder psychology: resilience, mental health, and the emotional journey
- Bootstrapping and capital-efficient startups
- The E-Myth: why most small businesses fail and what to do about it
- Indie hacking and solo founders
- The hard thing about hard things: navigating the toughest challenges

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **The Lean Startup** — Eric Ries: How to build a startup through rapid experimentation and validated learning.
- **Zero to One** — Peter Thiel: Notes on startups, or how to build the future — think differently, create new things.
- **The Hard Thing About Hard Things** — Ben Horowitz: Building a business when there are no easy answers.
- **The E-Myth Revisited** — Michael Gerber: Why most small businesses don't work and what to do about it.
- **Founders at Work** — Jessica Livingston: Interviews with the founders of the most successful tech companies.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Entrepreneurship is the engine of economic growth and innovation. These books provide the frameworks, stories, and practical advice that can mean the difference between a startup that succeeds and one that fails.
