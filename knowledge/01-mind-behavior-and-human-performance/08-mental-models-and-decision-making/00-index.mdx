# 🎯 Mental Models & Decision-Making

## 📚 Overview

The world is complex. Mental models are the cognitive tools that help us navigate that complexity. From first principles thinking to inversion, from Occam's razor to second-order thinking, these frameworks help us make better decisions and avoid common thinking traps.

This shelf collects the best books on mental models, decision-making frameworks, and the art of clear thinking. It includes Shane Parrish's Great Mental Models series, Annie Duke's work on decision-making under uncertainty, and Chip Heath's WRAP framework.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Cognitive Biases & Rationality** covers the errors in judgment; this category covers the tools for better judgment.
- **Systems Thinking & Game Theory** covers strategic interaction and feedback loops.
- **Probability & Forecasting** covers the mathematical side of uncertainty.

This category is about **the tools and frameworks** that help you think clearly and decide well.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Great mental models: first principles, inversion, Occam's razor, circle of competence
- Decision quality: framing, gathering information, reasoning, and commitment
- Thinking in bets: making decisions under uncertainty
- The paradox of choice: why more options can lead to worse outcomes
- Nudge and choice architecture
- Algorithms to live by: computer science applied to human decisions

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Great Mental Models Vol. 1** — Shane Parrish: The multidisciplinary thinking tools used by the world's best decision-makers.
- **Thinking in Bets** — Annie Duke: A former poker champion's framework for making better decisions under uncertainty.
- **Decisive** — Chip Heath: A four-step framework for overcoming the biases that lead to bad decisions.
- **Algorithms to Live By** — Brian Christian: How computer science can illuminate human decision-making.
- **Nudge** — Richard Thaler: How small changes in choice architecture can dramatically improve decisions.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

The quality of your decisions determines the quality of your life. Mental models are the cognitive tools that help you think clearly about complex problems, avoid common traps, and make choices you won't regret. This is the most practical shelf in the entire library.
