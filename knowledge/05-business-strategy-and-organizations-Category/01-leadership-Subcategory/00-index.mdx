# 👑 Leadership

## 📚 Overview

Leadership is the most studied and most debated topic in management. This shelf covers the major approaches: from Simon Sinek's servant leadership to Jocko Willink's extreme ownership, from Brené Brown's vulnerable leadership to Kim Scott's radical candor, from Patrick Lencioni's five dysfunctions to David Marquet's turn the ship around.

These books provide both the philosophy and the practical tools for leading people effectively.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Management & Organizational Behavior** covers the management of organizations.
- **Entrepreneurship & Startups** covers leading startups.
- **Business Strategy** covers strategic leadership.

This category focuses on the **art and practice of leading people** — inspiring, motivating, and guiding teams to achieve extraordinary results.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Servant leadership: leaders eat last
- Extreme ownership: taking responsibility for everything
- Radical candor: caring personally while challenging directly
- Vulnerability and courage in leadership
- The five dysfunctions of a team
- Multipliers: how the best leaders make everyone smarter
- The first 90 days: critical success strategies for new leaders
- Turn the ship around: a turning-leaders-into-followers story

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Leaders Eat Last** — Simon Sinek: Why some teams pull together and others don't — the biology of leadership.
- **Extreme Ownership** — Jocko Willink: How Navy SEALs lead and win — take complete responsibility for everything.
- **Radical Candor** — Kim Scott: Be a kick-ass boss without losing your humanity.
- **Dare to Lead** — Brené Brown: Brave work, tough conversations, whole hearts — leadership requires vulnerability.
- **The Five Dysfunctions of a Team** — Patrick Lencioni: A leadership fable about the deadly sins of teamwork.
