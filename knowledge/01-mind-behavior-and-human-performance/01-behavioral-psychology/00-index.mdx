# 🧠 Behavioral Psychology

## 📚 Overview

Behavioral psychology is the science of observable behavior. It asks: What shapes how we act? How do we learn new behaviors, and how do we unlearn maladaptive ones? From Pavlov's dogs to modern applied behavior analysis, this field has produced some of the most practical and evidence-based tools for understanding and changing behavior.

This shelf covers the full spectrum — classical and operant conditioning, child and adolescent development, behavior modification techniques, and the neuroscience that underlies it all.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Clinical Psychology** takes the diagnostic and therapeutic side — DSM classifications, psychotherapy modalities, and trauma treatment.
- **Cognitive & Behavioral Psychology** focuses on internal mental processes — memory, attention, language, and perception.
- **Social Psychology** covers group dynamics, conformity, and interpersonal influence.

This category stays focused on **learning, development, and behavior change** — the mechanisms by which experience shapes action.

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **The Whole-Brain Child** — Daniel Siegel: A revolutionary guide to how children's brains develop and how parents can nurture emotional intelligence.
- **The Boy Who Was Raised as a Dog** — Bruce Perry: Heartbreaking case studies showing how trauma reshapes the developing brain.
- **No Drama Discipline** — Siegel & Bryson: Evidence-based strategies for disciplining children without triggering power struggles.
- **How Children Succeed** — Paul Tough: Why grit, curiosity, and character matter more than IQ.
- **The Gardener and the Carpenter** — Alison Gopnik: A developmental psychologist's case for letting children learn through play.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Behavioral psychology is where theory meets practice. Its principles are used in every classroom, every parenting decision, every addiction treatment program, and every organizational change initiative. Understanding how behavior is shaped gives you the power to change it — in yourself and others.
