# 📊 Behavioral Finance

## 📚 Overview

Investors are not rational. We panic in crashes, get euphoric in bubbles, overtrade, chase performance, and let emotions override analysis. Behavioral finance studies these patterns — and provides the tools to overcome them.

This shelf includes Nassim Taleb's black swan theory, Morgan Housel's psychology of money, Charlie Munger's Poor Charlie's Almanack, and Richard Thaler's Misbehaving. It's the essential reading for anyone who invests — or wants to understand why markets behave the way they do.

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **The Psychology of Money** — Morgan Housel: Timeless lessons on wealth, greed, and happiness — why being "right" about money has less to do with intelligence and more to do with behavior.
- **The Black Swan** — Nassim Taleb: How rare, unpredictable events dominate history and markets — and why we consistently fail to anticipate them.
- **Poor Charlie's Almanack** — Charlie Munger: The wit and wisdom of Warren Buffett's partner, packed with mental models for better thinking.
- **Misbehaving** — Richard Thaler: The history of behavioral economics by one of its founders.
- **Fooled by Randomness** — Nassim Taleb: How we consistently underestimate the role of luck in success.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

The biggest threat to your investment returns isn't the market — it's your own behavior. These books will help you understand the psychological traps that lead to bad investment decisions and provide the frameworks to invest more wisely.
