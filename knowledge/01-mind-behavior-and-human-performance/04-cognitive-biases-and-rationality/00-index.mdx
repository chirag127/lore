# ⚖️ Cognitive Biases & Rationality

## 📚 Overview

Human judgment is systematically flawed. We overestimate our abilities, see patterns in noise, prefer confirming evidence, and make decisions based on gut feelings that lead us astray. Cognitive biases aren't bugs — they're features of a brain optimized for survival, not accuracy.

This shelf catalogs the major biases, explores their origins, and provides practical debiasing strategies. It includes some of the most important books ever written about human irrationality and how to overcome it.

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Thinking, Fast and Slow** — Daniel Kahneman: The definitive work on the cognitive biases that shape our judgments.
- **Factfulness** — Hans Rosling: A data-driven antidote to the dramatic worldview that distorts our perception of reality.
- **Scout Mindset** — Julia Galef: Why some people see things clearly and others don't — and how to join the first group.
- **The Power of Habit** — Charles Duhigg: How habits form, how they shape our behavior, and how to change them.
- **Debiasing Strategies and Decision Hygiene** — Practical tools for improving the quality of your judgments.

Every decision you make — from what to eat to whom to vote for — is filtered through cognitive biases. Learning to recognize and counteract them is perhaps the highest-ROI mental skill you can develop. These books will make you a clearer thinker, a better decision-maker, and a more rational human being.
