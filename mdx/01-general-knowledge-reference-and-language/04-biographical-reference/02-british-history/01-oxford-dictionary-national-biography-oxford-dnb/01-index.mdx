# The Oxford Dictionary of National Biography

## Executive Summary

The Oxford Dictionary of National Biography is the national record of men and women who have shaped British history and culture. With over 60,000 biographies written by more than 10,000 specialist contributors, it is the largest and most authoritative biographical reference work in the English language. The 2004 edition thoroughly revised and expanded the original Dictionary of National Biography (1885-1900), extending coverage to include women, non-elite figures, and the full span of British history from prehistoric times to the 21st century.



## Key Takeaways

- **Covers everyone** from kings to obscure inventors
- **Each entry** is written by a specialist scholar with critical assessment
- **The Oxford DNB (2004)** is a complete revision of the original 1885-1900 work
- **Entries include** a bibliography for further reading
- **The online edition** is updated quarterly with new biographies

## Who Should Read

- **Academic researchers**: Authoritative biographical source
- **History enthusiasts**: British history through biography
- **Genealogists**: Family history research
- **Fact-checkers**: Verify biographical facts about British figures

## Who Should Skip

- Those needing **non-British** figures
- Casual readers wanting **narrative biographies**
- Those without access to **a research library** or subscription

## Difficulty

**Hard** — Scholarly reference work. Entries vary in accessibility but are written for an academic audience.
