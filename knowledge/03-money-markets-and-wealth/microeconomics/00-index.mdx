# 🔬 Microeconomics

## 📚 Overview

Microeconomics is the study of how individual agents — consumers, firms, workers — make decisions and interact in markets. It's the foundation of economic thinking: supply and demand, price theory, market structures, and strategic behavior.

This shelf covers the core principles of microeconomics and their applications to business strategy, market design, and public policy.

| Included | Excluded |
|----------|----------|
| Price theory and supply/demand | Macroeconomics (→ Macroeconomics) |

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Macroeconomics** covers the economy as a whole.
- **Finance** covers financial markets.
- **Business Strategy** covers the application of microeconomic principles to business.

This category focuses on the **foundations of market behavior** — how prices are set, how firms compete, and how consumers choose.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Price theory: supply, demand, and equilibrium
- Market structures: perfect competition, monopoly, oligopoly
- Consumer behavior and utility maximization
- Firm behavior and profit maximization
- Strategic interaction in markets

## ⭐ Essential Books

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Microeconomics is the foundation of rational decision-making. Understanding how markets work — prices, competition, incentives — is essential for anyone in business, policy, or everyday life.
