# 🙏 Religion & Spirituality

## 📚 Overview

Religion and spirituality are among the most powerful forces in human life. This shelf covers the world's major traditions: Hinduism, Buddhism, Christianity, Islam, Judaism, Jainism, Sikhism, Taoism, Confucianism, Shinto, and the broader landscape of mysticism, interfaith dialogue, and spirituality without religion.

With over 30 books, this is one of the most comprehensive collections in BookAtlas. It includes Karen Armstrong's histories of God and the Bible, Huston Smith's World's Religions, the Bhagavad Gita, the Tao Te Ching, and Richard Dawkins's God Delusion.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Philosophy** covers the philosophy of religion.
- **Eastern Philosophy** covers the philosophical dimensions of Eastern traditions.
- **Ethics & Moral Philosophy** covers religious ethics.

This category focuses on the **religious and spiritual traditions** themselves — their texts, practices, histories, and lived experiences.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Hinduism: the Bhagavad Gita, Vedanta, mysticism, and tantra
- Buddhism: the Dharma, mindfulness, and meditation
- Christianity: the Bible, mysticism, and history
- Islam: the Quran, Sufi mysticism, and Islamic thought
- Judaism: the Torah, Jewish mysticism, and history
- Jainism, Sikhism, Taoism, Confucianism, Shinto
- Interfaith dialogue and comparative religion
- Spirituality without religion
- Death, impermanence, and living fully
- Finding purpose and meaning

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **A History of God** — Karen Armstrong: A 4,000-year history of the concept of God in Judaism, Christianity, and Islam.
- **The World's Religions** — Huston Smith: A classic introduction to the world's major religious traditions.
- **The Bhagavad Gita** — The Hindu scripture that is one of the most influential spiritual texts ever written.
- **The God Delusion** — Richard Dawkins: A provocative critique of religious belief from a scientific perspective.
- **Zen in the Art of Archery** — Eugen Herrigel: A Westerner's journey into Zen through the practice of archery.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Religion and spirituality shape the lives of billions of people. Understanding these traditions — their texts, practices, and histories — is essential for anyone who wants to understand the human condition.
