# 🎯 Business Strategy

## 📚 Overview

Strategy is the art of making choices: where to play, how to win, and what to do differently from competitors. This shelf covers the major frameworks and thinkers in business strategy: from Michael Porter's competitive strategy to Kim & Mauborgne's blue ocean, from Jim Collins's Built to Last to Richard Rumelt's Good Strategy Bad Strategy.

These books are essential for anyone who leads a business, advises businesses, or wants to understand how companies create and sustain competitive advantage.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Entrepreneurship & Startups** covers starting new businesses.
- **Leadership** covers leading people and organizations.

This category focuses on **corporate and competitive strategy** — how established businesses create and sustain advantage.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Competitive strategy: industry analysis, positioning, differentiation
- Blue ocean strategy: creating uncontested market space
- Built to last: visionary companies and their enduring practices
- Good strategy vs. bad strategy: the kernel of good strategy
- Corporate diversification and portfolio strategy

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Good Strategy Bad Strategy** — Richard Rumelt: Why most strategy is bad, and what good strategy looks like.
- **Blue Ocean Strategy** — Kim & Mauborgne: How to create uncontested market space and make competition irrelevant.
- **Built to Last** — Jim Collins: The habits of visionary companies that have endured for decades.
- **Competitive Strategy** — Michael Porter: The foundational framework for analyzing industries and competitors.
- **Blue Ocean Strategy (Chan Kim)** — The expanded edition with new tools and frameworks.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Strategy is the most important function of leadership. Without a clear strategy, organizations drift, waste resources, and lose to competitors who know where they're going. These books provide the frameworks for making strategic choices that create lasting advantage.
