# 🎭 Drama & Theatre

## 📚 Overview

Drama is one of the oldest art forms. This shelf covers the full spectrum: from classical Greek and Roman drama to Renaissance and Elizabethan theatre, from modern drama to contemporary playwriting, from dance theory to opera and musical theatre.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Film & Media Studies** covers film and cinema.
- **Poetry & Drama** covers the literary analysis of dramatic texts.
- **Literary Criticism** covers the theory of dramatic criticism.

This category focuses on **performance** — drama as a living, staged art.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Classical drama: Greek tragedy and Roman comedy
- Renaissance and Elizabethan drama: Shakespeare and his contemporaries
- Modern drama: Ibsen, Chekhov, Beckett, and beyond
- Contemporary playwriting
- Dance theory and choreography
- Opera, musical theatre, and performance art
- Film studies and cinema theory

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Classical Drama** — The foundations of Western drama: Aeschylus, Sophocles, Euripides, Aristophanes.
- **Renaissance and Elizabethan Drama** — Shakespeare, Marlowe, and the golden age of English theatre.
- **Modern Drama** — Ibsen, Chekhov, Beckett, and the revolution in 20th-century theatre.
- **Contemporary Playwriting** — The art and craft of writing for the modern stage.
- **Dance Theory and Choreography** — The principles of movement and performance.

## 🗺 Related Categories

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Drama is the art of human conflict and resolution. Understanding it gives you insight into the human condition and the power of live performance to move, challenge, and transform audiences.
