# 🌍 Geopolitics & Great Power Competition

## 📚 Overview

Geopolitics is the study of how geography, economics, and military power shape international relations. This shelf covers the great themes: the geography of power, the clash of civilizations, US-China competition, and India's role in the global order.

These books provide the frameworks for understanding the most consequential power dynamics of the 21st century.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **International Relations** covers diplomatic history and international organizations.
- **Political Science & Geopolitics** covers domestic political systems.
- **Macroeconomics** covers economic competition between nations.

This category focuses on the **strategic competition** among great powers.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Geography, power, and national interest
- US-China competition: the defining rivalry of the 21st century
- The clash of civilizations and the remaking of world order
- India's role in the global order

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **The Clash of Civilizations** — Samuel Huntington: The provocative thesis that cultural and civilizational identities will be the primary source of conflict in the post-Cold War world.
- **US-China Competition** — The defining geopolitical rivalry of the 21st century and its implications for the world.
- **Geography, Power, and National Interest** — How the physical world shapes the political world.
- **India in the Global Order** — India's rising power and its implications for the international system.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

We live in an era of great power competition. Understanding geopolitics is essential for making sense of international news, business strategy, and the forces that will shape the 21st century.
