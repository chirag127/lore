First published in 1940 and revised in 1972, *How to Read a Book* is the definitive guide to what its subtitle calls "intelligent reading." Adler and Van Doren argue that reading is not a passive act of consumption but a skilled activity requiring technique, discipline, and active engagement with the text.

The book introduces four levels of reading that build on each other: elementary reading (basic literacy), inspectional reading (quickly grasping a book's structure and significance), analytical reading (thorough, systematic engagement with a single work), and syntopical reading (reading multiple books on the same subject to develop a comprehensive understanding). For anyone building a personal knowledge management system, this book provides the methodology for extracting maximum value from every book you read.
