# A History of the World in 100 Objects

## Executive Summary

Neil MacGregor, then director of the British Museum, selects 100 objects from the museum's collection to tell a history of humanity. Spanning two million years from the earliest stone tools to a 2008 credit card, each object opens a window into the culture that produced it. The book originated as a BBC Radio 4 series and became a landmark of accessible world history, demonstrating that material objects can reveal aspects of human experience that written history misses.



## Key Takeaways

- **Objects reveal** aspects of past lives that written records omit
- **Every object carries** multiple stories — creation, use, and discovery
- **The selection spans** 2 million years across all continents
- **Originated as** a BBC Radio 4 series
- **Objects range** from humble everyday items to great works of art

## Who Should Read

- **History fans**: Fresh perspective on world history
- **Museum lovers**: Deep dive into British Museum collection
- **Narrative nonfiction fans**: Engaging object-based stories
- **Teachers**: Engaging way to present history

## Who Should Skip

- Those seeking **traditional political or military** history
- Readers wanting **deep coverage** of specific periods
- People who want **text-only** history without visual illustration

## Difficulty

**Easy** — Accessible to any reader. Each object chapter is short and self-contained.

## Reading Time

- **Full Read**: 10-12 hours
- **Per Object**: 5-10 minutes
