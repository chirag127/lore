# The Little Book of Remarkable Facts

## Executive Summary

Bob Morse's The Little Book of Remarkable Facts is a charming collection of surprising, curious, and little-known facts organized into thematic categories. From science and nature to history and everyday life, each fact is designed to spark wonder and conversation. The book is perfect for browsing, offering a delightful reminder that the world is full of extraordinary things we never knew.



## Key Takeaways

- **Covers facts** from science, history, nature, and everyday life
- **Each fact** is concise, surprising, and memorable
- **Collection designed** for browsing and casual reading
- **Facts organized** into thematic categories
- **Tone** is lighthearted and wonder-filled

## Who Should Read

- **Curious minds**: Surprising facts about the world
- **Conversation starters**: Social icebreakers
- **Teachers**: Engaging classroom material
- **Wonder seekers**: Appreciate the extraordinary in everyday life

## Who Should Skip

- Those seeking **systematic reference** works
- Readers who prefer **in-depth analysis** to isolated facts
- People who find **trivia collections** trivial

## Difficulty

**Easy** — Accessible to all readers. Each fact is self-contained.

## Reading Time

- **Full Read**: 2-3 hours
- **Per Entry**: 30 seconds
