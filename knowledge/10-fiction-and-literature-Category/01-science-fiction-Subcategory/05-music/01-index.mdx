# 🎵 Music

## 📚 Overview

Music is the most universal of all art forms. This shelf covers the full spectrum: from music theory and harmony to the history of Western music, from music psychology and neuroscience to the music industry and recording technology.

With over 13 books, this is one of the richest music categories in BookAtlas. It includes Oliver Sacks's Musicophilia, Daniel Levitin's This Is Your Brain on Music, Alex Ross's The Rest Is Noise, and David Byrne's How Music Works.

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Musicophilia** — Oliver Sacks: Tales of music and the brain — how music affects the mind and what it reveals about human nature.
- **This Is Your Brain on Music** — Daniel Levitin: A neuroscientist's exploration of how music affects the brain.
- **The Rest Is Noise** — Alex Ross: A history of 20th-century classical music and its cultural context.
- **How Music Works** — David Byrne: The Talking Heads frontman's exploration of music's contexts — how it's created, performed, and experienced.
- **Music Theory and Harmony** — The foundations of how music works.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Music is the art form that most directly touches the emotions. Understanding how it works — its theory, history, psychology, and cultural context — deepens your appreciation and helps you create better music.
