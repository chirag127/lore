# 📐 Computer Science Theory

## 📚 Overview

Beneath every practical system lies a theoretical foundation. This shelf covers the theoretical pillars of computer science: how compilers translate high-level code to machine instructions, how information theory quantifies data, how compression algorithms shrink files, and how error-correcting codes ensure reliable communication.

These books are for the engineer who wants to understand not just how things work, but why they work — and what the fundamental limits are.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Theory of Computation** covers computability and complexity theory.
- **Data & Algorithms** covers algorithm design and analysis.
- **Computer Science** covers programming language theory.

This category focuses on the **mathematical and theoretical** foundations that enable practical computing.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Compiler design: lexical analysis, parsing, code generation
- Intermediate representations and optimization passes
- Shannon's information theory
- Data compression theory
- Error-correcting codes
- Code generation and runtime systems

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Compilers** — Aho, Lam, Sethi, Ullman: The "Dragon Book" — the definitive textbook on compiler construction.
- **Shannon's Information Theory** — The mathematical foundation of data communication and compression.
- **Data Compression Theory** — How algorithms like Huffman coding and LZW shrink data.
- **Error-Correcting Codes** — How to detect and correct errors in data transmission and storage.
- **Lexical Analysis and Parsing** — How compilers understand the structure of code.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Theoretical computer science is the foundation of every practical system. Understanding compilers, information theory, and error-correcting codes gives you a deeper appreciation for the engineering that makes modern computing possible.
