# 📊 Corporate Finance & Accounting

## 📚 Overview

Corporate finance is the language of business. This shelf covers the core topics: how to read and analyze financial statements, how to value companies, and how mergers and acquisitions work.

Whether you're an entrepreneur, investor, or manager, understanding corporate finance is essential for making informed business decisions.

## 🎯 Scope

| Included | Excluded |
|----------|----------|
| Financial statement analysis | Personal finance (→ Personal Finance) |
| Corporate valuation | Investment theory (→ Investment Theory) |
| Mergers and acquisitions | Financial markets (→ Finance) |

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Personal Finance** covers individual money management.
- **Investment Theory** covers investment analysis and portfolio management.
- **Finance** covers financial instruments and markets.

This category focuses on the **financial management of corporations** — how businesses raise capital, allocate resources, and create value.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Financial statements: income statement, balance sheet, cash flow statement
- Corporate valuation methods: DCF, comparable transactions, precedent deals
- Mergers and acquisitions: deal structure, due diligence, integration
- Capital structure and financing decisions

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Financial Statements** — How to read, analyze, and interpret the three core financial statements.
- **Corporate Valuation** — The methods and models used to value businesses.
- **Mergers and Acquisitions** — How M&A deals work, from strategy to integration.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Corporate finance is the language of business. Understanding financial statements, valuation, and M&A is essential for anyone who wants to lead a business, invest in businesses, or understand how value is created and destroyed.
