# 🌟 Positive Psychology

## 📚 Overview

For most of its history, psychology focused on what goes wrong — mental illness, dysfunction, and suffering. Positive psychology asks a different question: What goes right? What makes people thrive, flourish, and live meaningful lives?

This shelf covers the science of well-being — from Martin Seligman's PERMA model to Jonathan Haidt's happiness hypothesis, from flow states to post-traumatic growth. It's the science of building a life worth living.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Clinical Psychology** covers the treatment of mental illness; this category covers the promotion of mental health.
- **Personality Psychology** covers stable traits; this category covers cultivatable strengths.
- **Social Psychology** covers relationships; this category covers individual flourishing.

This category is about **what makes life good** — not just the absence of illness, but the presence of well-being.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- **Flow, Meaning, and Eudaimonia** — The psychology of optimal experience and a life well-lived.
- **Character Strengths and Virtues** — The VIA classification of human strengths and how to cultivate them.
