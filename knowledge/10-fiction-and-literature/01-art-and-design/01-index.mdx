# 🎨 Art & Design

## 📚 Overview

Design is everywhere — in the products we use, the buildings we inhabit, and the interfaces we interact with. This shelf covers the principles and practice of visual design: from Don Norman's Design of Everyday Things to Steve Krug's Don't Make Me Think, from Alan Cooper's About Face to Austin Kleon's Steal Like an Artist.

With over 25 books, this is one of the richest categories in BookAtlas. It covers UI/UX design, graphic design, industrial design, architecture, photography, art history, and the principles that make design work.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Literary Criticism** covers the analysis of literary texts.
- **Music** covers music theory and history.

This category focuses on **visual design** in all its forms.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- The Design of Everyday Things: why some products work and others don't
- Don't Make Me Think: web usability and user experience
- About Face: the essentials of interaction design
- Steal Like an Artist: creativity in the age of the internet

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **The Design of Everyday Things** — Don Norman: Why some products delight and others frustrate — the principles of human-centered design.
- **Don't Make Me Think** — Steve Krug: A common sense approach to web usability that every designer should read.
- **About Face** — Alan Cooper: The essential guide to interaction design.
- **Steal Like an Artist** — Austin Kleon: How to unlock your creativity by embracing influence and imitation.
- **Thinking with Type** — Ellen Lupton: The definitive guide to typography for designers.
