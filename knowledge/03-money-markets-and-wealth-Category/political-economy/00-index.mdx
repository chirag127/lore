# ⚖️ Political Economy

## 📚 Overview

Political economy studies the relationship between economics and political power. How do institutions shape economic outcomes? When do markets fail, and what should government do about it? How do we price carbon, manage externalities, and pursue sustainable development?

This shelf covers the intersection of economics and politics — the questions that neither economists nor politicians can answer alone.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Macroeconomics** and **Microeconomics** cover pure economic theory.
- **International Relations** covers geopolitical dimensions.
- **Public Policy & Law** covers the legal and regulatory framework.

This category focuses on the **political dimensions of economic life** — power, institutions, and the rules that govern markets.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

The biggest economic challenges of our time — climate change, inequality, financial instability — are also political challenges. Understanding political economy is essential for anyone who wants to shape a more just and sustainable world.
