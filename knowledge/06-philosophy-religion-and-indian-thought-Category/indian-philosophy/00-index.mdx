# 🕉️ Indian Philosophy

## 📚 Overview

India has one of the richest philosophical traditions in human history. This shelf covers the major schools: the six orthodox (astika) schools including Advaita Vedanta and Vishishtadvaita, the heterodox (nastika) schools including Buddhism and Jainism, Indian logic (Navya-Nyaya), and modern Indian philosophy.

These books provide access to a philosophical tradition that is as rigorous and sophisticated as any in the West — and in many ways, more comprehensive.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Advaita Vedanta: non-dual philosophy
- Dvaita and Vishishtadvaita: dualist and qualified non-dualist schools
- Buddhist philosophy: the Middle Way and dependent origination
- Jain philosophy: non-violence and the many-sidedness of truth
- Navya-Nyaya: the new school of Indian logic
- Six orthodox schools: Samkhya, Yoga, Nyaya, Vaisheshika, Mimamsa, Vedanta
- Heterodox schools: Buddhism, Jainism, Charvaka
- Modern Indian philosophy

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Advaita Vedanta** — The non-dual philosophy that teaches the identity of Atman (self) and Brahman (ultimate reality).
- **Buddhist Philosophy** — The Middle Way, dependent origination, and the path to liberation.
- **Six Orthodox Schools** — A systematic overview of the six major schools of Hindu philosophy.
- **Dvaita and Vishishtadvaita** — The dualist and qualified non-dualist alternatives to Advaita.
- **Indian Logic (Navya-Nyaya)** — The sophisticated system of Indian logic and epistemology.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Indian philosophy offers some of the most profound and systematic thinking about consciousness, reality, and the nature of the self. It's a tradition that deserves to be studied alongside — not subordinate to — Western philosophy.
