# 👥 Social Psychology

## 📚 Overview

Humans are fundamentally social creatures. Our thoughts, feelings, and behaviors are profoundly shaped by the people around us — even when we're alone. Social psychology studies these influences scientifically.

This shelf covers the major findings in social psychology — from Cialdini's principles of influence to Milgram's obedience experiments, from group dynamics to romantic relationships, from prejudice to prosocial behavior. It includes some of the most influential psychology books ever written.

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Personality Psychology** covers stable individual differences; this category covers situational social influences.
- **Clinical Psychology** covers social anxiety and related disorders.
- **Industrial-Organizational Psychology** covers social dynamics in workplace settings.

This category is about **how people affect each other** — the social forces that shape human behavior.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Influence and persuasion (Cialdini's 7 principles)
- Pre-suasion: the art of setting the stage
- Group dynamics, conformity, and obedience
- Prejudice, discrimination, and intergroup relations
- Prosocial behavior and altruism
- Romantic relationships and attachment theory
- Emotional intelligence and interpersonal skills
- Building deep friendships
- The psychology of moral and political disagreement

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Influence** — Robert Cialdini: The classic work on the psychology of persuasion and why people say yes.
- **Pre-Suasion** — Robert Cialdini: How to set the stage for persuasion before you make your request.
- **Emotional Intelligence** — Daniel Goleman: Why EQ matters more than IQ for success in life and work.
- **The Righteous Mind** — Jonathan Haidt: Why good people are divided by politics and religion.
- **The Charisma Myth** — Olivia Fox Cabane: How anyone can develop the magnetic presence of charismatic people.

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Every interaction you have — from a sales pitch to a first date, from a team meeting to a political debate — is shaped by social psychology. Understanding these forces helps you influence ethically, resist manipulation, build better relationships, and navigate the social world more effectively.
