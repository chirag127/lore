Science journalist Benedict Carey takes readers on an engaging tour of learning science, translating academic research into accessible stories and practical insights. The book's central message is liberating: many of the study habits we've been taught are wrong, and many of the things we think are bad for learning are actually essential.

Carey covers much of the same ground as *Make It Stick* but in a more narrative, less academic style. He explains why forgetting is not the enemy of learning but its ally, why sleeping on a problem actually works, and why the best way to learn is often counterintuitive. The book is organized around the idea that the brain is not a passive recording device but an active, interpretive system that learns best when challenged, interrupted, and given time to process.
