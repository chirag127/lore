# 🧬 Evolutionary Psychology

## 📚 Overview

Why do humans fear snakes but not cars? Why do we fall in love? Why are we cooperative with some people and competitive with others? Evolutionary psychology answers these questions by examining how natural selection shaped the human mind.

This shelf covers the evolutionary origins of human behavior — from mating strategies and cooperation to cross-cultural universals. It provides the deep "why" behind the patterns studied in other psychology categories.

## 🎯 Scope

| Included | Excluded |
|----------|----------|
| Evolutionary origins of behavior | Cognitive processes (→ Cognitive Psychology) |
| Mating, cooperation, and competition | Social influence (→ Social Psychology) |
| Cross-cultural universals | Child development (→ Behavioral Psychology) |
| Evolutionary explanations for modern behavior | Clinical disorders (→ Clinical Psychology) |

## 🔗 Category Boundaries

- **Cognitive Psychology** covers how the mind processes information, not why it evolved that way.
- **Social Psychology** examines social behavior in the present, not its evolutionary origins.
- **Behavioral Psychology** focuses on learning mechanisms shaped by evolution.

This category provides the **ultimate causation** — the evolutionary "why" — behind human behavior.

## 📖 Topics Covered

- Evolutionary origins of human behavior
- Mating strategies and sexual selection
- Cooperation, competition, and altruism
- Cross-cultural universals of human behavior
- The mismatch between our evolved nature and modern environments

## ⭐ Essential Books

- **Evolutionary Origins of Human Behavior** — A comprehensive overview of how natural selection shaped the human mind.
- **Mating, Cooperation, and Competition** — The evolutionary dynamics that drive social behavior.
- **Cross-Cultural Universals of Human Behavior** — What all humans share, regardless of culture, and what it reveals about our evolved nature.

## 🗺 Related Categories

- **Behavioral Psychology** — Learning and conditioning mechanisms
- **Social Psychology** — Group dynamics and interpersonal influence
- **Cognitive Biases & Rationality** — Biases as evolved heuristics
- **Biology & Evolution** (in Math, Logic & Science) — The biological basis of evolution

## 🚀 Why This Category Matters

Evolutionary psychology provides the deepest explanation for human behavior. Understanding our evolved nature helps us make sense of everything from relationships to politics, from consumer behavior to conflict. It's the ultimate "why" behind everything else in psychology.
