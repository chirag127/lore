The 10,000-hour rule from Malcolm Gladwell's *Outliers* has convinced millions that mastery requires a decade of practice. While that may be true for world-class expertise, it misses a crucial point: you do not need to be world-class to enjoy most skills. You just need to be good enough. And according to Josh Kaufman, you can reach "good enough" in just twenty hours.

Kaufman, author of the international bestseller *The Personal MBA*, developed a systematic method for rapid skill acquisition after realizing that the 10,000-hour figure was paralyzing people from even starting. His approach is simple: deconstruct the skill, learn just enough to self-correct, remove barriers to practice, and commit to twenty hours of focused effort. The initial frustration hump that stops most people in the first few hours is exactly where the steepest learning curve lies.

*The First 20 Hours* is part manifesto, part practical guide. Kaufman walks through his method and then demonstrates it with real examples—he learned to play the ukulele, program a web app, practice yoga, and more, each in about twenty hours. The book is an antidote to the intimidating idea that learning anything worthwhile requires a massive, decade-long commitment.
